
=== MODEL INFORMATION ===

Model: LightGBM binary classifier
Target: Probability of wildfire within 10-21 days per H3 hexagon (resolution 5, ~252 km²)
Training: 2.9M observations, 1,523 California hexagons, 2020–2025
Performance: ROC-AUC 0.892, AUC-PR 0.29 (14× above the 2% random baseline)
Base rate: 2.08% of hex-days have a fire within the forecast window

RISK LEVELS:
- NO RISK (<1%): Negligible fire probability.
- LOW (1–3%): At or below baseline seasonal risk. Routine monitoring.
- MODERATE (3–8%): Elevated conditions worth monitoring.
- HIGH (8–15%): Significant risk. Preparedness actions warranted.
- VERY HIGH (>15%): Extreme risk. Active response measures needed.

KNOWN LIMITATIONS:
- Geographic features dominate — model partially learns WHERE fires historically occur.
- Class imbalance 1:47 — false alarms expected.
- California only.

=== EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT REFERENCE ===

KEY INDICATORS:
- NDVI below 0.20: severe vegetation dryness
- Daytime LST above 42°C: extreme heat stress
- Wind gusts above 35 mph: rapid fire spread
- Santa Ana winds (hot, dry, NE) in Southern CA: particularly dangerous

CAL FIRE READY-SET-GO:
- READY: Create defensible space, prepare go-bags, know evacuation routes.
- SET: Evacuation warning. Be prepared to leave immediately.
- GO: Evacuation order. Leave immediately.

RESOURCE POSITIONING:
- Type 1 engines within 15-min response time of high-risk areas.
- Aerial resources on standby at nearest air base.
- Water tenders at key highway corridors.

HISTORICAL:
- CA fire season peaks July–November; Santa Ana events Oct–Dec.
- 2020 August Complex Fire: 1M+ acres.
- 2018 Camp Fire: 85 fatalities, 40+ mph winds.
