
=== SYSTEM INFORMATION ===

Prediction system: Random Forest classifier (181 trees)
Target: Probability of wildfire within the next 10–20 days per zone (~252 km²)
Training: 2.9M observations across 1,523 California zones, 2020–2025
Performance: ROC-AUC 0.895, best operational F1 0.648 (10–21 day window)
Base rate: 2.08% of zone-days have a fire within the forecast window

RISK LEVELS:
- NONE (0–12.7%): Background risk. No action needed.
- LOW (12.7–28.2%): Slightly elevated. Monitor conditions.
- MODERATE (28.2–50.7%): Meaningful risk. Heightened awareness, stage resources.
- HIGH (50.7–76.6%): Serious risk. Active preparedness, pre-position crews.
- VERY HIGH (76.6–100%): Extreme risk. Full activation, issue warnings.

Operational threshold: 28.2% — zones above this are flagged for action.

KNOWN LIMITATIONS:
- Geographic features (latitude/longitude) are strong predictors — the system partially
  learns WHERE fires historically occur, not just current conditions.
- False alarms expected due to 1:47 class imbalance.
- California only. Not generalizable without retraining.
- Satellite vegetation data has 8–16 day composite cadences.

=== EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT REFERENCE ===

KEY INDICATORS:
- Vegetation dryness (NDVI below 0.20): severe fuel dryness
- Vapor Pressure Deficit (VPD) above 4 kPa: atmosphere extremely dry
- Daytime surface temperature above 42°C: extreme heat stress
- Wind gusts above 35 mph: rapid fire spread
- Dry streak above 30 days: prolonged absence of precipitation
- Santa Ana winds (hot, dry, NE) in Southern CA: particularly dangerous

CAL FIRE READY-SET-GO:
- READY: Create defensible space, prepare go-bags, know evacuation routes.
- SET: Evacuation warning. Be prepared to leave immediately.
- GO: Evacuation order. Leave immediately.

RESOURCE POSITIONING:
- Type 1 engines within 15-min response time of high-risk areas.
- Aerial resources on standby at nearest air base.
- Water tenders at key highway corridors.

HISTORICAL:
- CA fire season peaks July–November; Santa Ana events Oct–Dec.
- 2020 August Complex Fire: 1M+ acres.
- 2018 Camp Fire: 85 fatalities, 40+ mph winds.
