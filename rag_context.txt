
=== MODEL INFORMATION ===

Model: LightGBM binary classifier
Target: Probability of wildfire within 14 days per H3 hexagon (resolution 5, ~252 km²)
Training: 2.9M observations, 1,523 California hexagons, 2020–2025
Performance: ROC-AUC 0.892, AUC-PR 0.29 (14× above the 2% random baseline)
Base rate: 2.08% of hex-days have a fire within 14 days

RISK LEVELS (based on predicted probability):
- LOW (0–3%): At or below baseline seasonal risk. Routine monitoring.
- MODERATE (3–8%): Elevated conditions worth monitoring. 1.5–4× baseline.
- HIGH (8–15%): Significant risk. Preparedness actions warranted. 4–7× baseline.
- VERY HIGH (>15%): Extreme risk. Active response measures needed. 7×+ baseline.

KNOWN MODEL LIMITATIONS (be transparent about these):
- Geographic features (lat/lon) are among the strongest predictors — the model
  partially learns WHERE fires historically occur, not just conditions.
- 14-day horizon is long; model captures seasonal and geographic patterns best.
- Class imbalance 1:47 — false alarms are expected. A "VERY HIGH" hex has maybe
  a 15-50% chance of fire, not near-certainty.
- California only. Not generalizable without retraining.
- MODIS satellite data has multi-day composite cadences (not real-time).

FEATURES (31 total):
- Weather: max/min temperature, wind speed, precipitation, elevation
- Derived: 7-day and 30-day rolling averages, lag features, drought index
- Satellite (MODIS): NDVI, EVI, LAI, FPAR, land surface temperature (day/night)
- Temporal: month, day of year, season

=== WILDFIRE EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT REFERENCE ===

KEY ENVIRONMENTAL INDICATORS:
- NDVI below 0.20: severe vegetation dryness, high fuel availability
- Daytime land surface temperature above 42°C: extreme heat stress
- Nighttime LST above 25°C: vegetation cannot recover moisture overnight
- Wind gusts above 35 mph: significantly increases fire spread rate
- Relative humidity below 15%: critical fire weather conditions
- Santa Ana winds (hot, dry, northeast) in Southern CA: particularly dangerous
- Drought index rising: prolonged precipitation deficit drying fuel loads

CAL FIRE READY-SET-GO FRAMEWORK:
- READY: Create defensible space, prepare go-bags, know evacuation routes.
- SET: Evacuation warning issued. Be prepared to leave immediately.
- GO: Evacuation order issued. Leave immediately via designated routes.

RESOURCE PRE-POSITIONING GUIDELINES:
- Type 1 engines within 15-minute response time of high-risk areas.
- Aerial resources (helicopters, air tankers) on standby at nearest air base.
- Water tenders staged at key highway corridors near wildland-urban interface.
- Incident Management Teams on alert for potential deployment.

MUTUAL AID:
- California Master Mutual Aid Agreement: counties request cross-county assistance.
- Federal resources (USFS, BLM) available through NIFC coordination.

COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS:
- Red Flag Warnings: issued by NWS for critical fire weather.
- Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA): imminent evacuation orders.
- Community notification via Nixle, AlertCA, local emergency apps.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT:
- CA fire season peaks July–November; Santa Ana events October–December.
- 2020 August Complex Fire: 1M+ acres, largest in CA history.
- 2018 Camp Fire (Paradise): 85 fatalities, 40+ mph winds through dense WUI.
